# Introduction

**Come** is a basic verb, but when it pairs with a little preposition or adverb, the meaning can change completely.  
In this post I focus on six high‑frequency patterns—**come in, come out, come up, come across, come over, come along**—and show you exactly how to use them in conversation.  
Read the explanations, and you’ll be well on your way to mastering these “come” phrases.

---

# Learning

## come in

> **Meaning**: Enter a room or place.  
> **日本語**：入る、どうぞ入って。

### More Examples

1. **Come in** and have a seat.
2. She **came in** without knocking.
3. What time does the train **come in**?
4. “Can I **come in** now?”
5. Fresh air **comes in** through the window.
6. New data **came in** this morning.

**Tip:** At the doorway, a simple “**Come in!**” is the classic invitation.

---

## come out

> **Meaning**: Appear, be released, or become known.  
> **日本語**：出る、公開される、明らかになる。

### More Examples

1. The movie **comes out** next Friday.
2. The sun **came out** after the rain.
3. The truth finally **came out**.
4. How did the photos **come out**?
5. My book **came out** in March.
6. The stain won’t **come out**.

**Tip:** Ask about results with “**How did it come out?**”

---

## come up

> **Meaning**: Appear, be mentioned, or approach in time.  
> **日本語**：話題に上がる、近づく、急に起こる。

### More Examples

1. Your name **came up** at the meeting.
2. Something **came up**, so I can’t go.
3. The deadline is **coming up** soon.
4. A new idea **came up** while walking.
5. Exams are **coming up** next month.
6. Problems **come up** when you least expect.

**Tip:** Use **come up** for last‑minute issues or events that are just around the corner.

---

## come across

> **Meaning**: Find by chance or give an impression.  
> **日本語**：偶然見つける、（人柄が）伝わる。

### More Examples

1. I **came across** an old photo.
2. She **comes across** as confident.
3. He **came across** a great café.
4. Your email **came across** clearly.
5. They **came across** some rare coins.
6. His joke didn’t **come across** well.

**Tip:** **come across as + adjective** talks about the impression you give: “He **comes across as** friendly.”

---

## come over

> **Meaning**: Visit informally or move from one side to another.  
> **日本語**：ふらっと訪ねる、こちらへ来る。

### More Examples

1. **Come over** for dinner tonight.
2. She **came over** to my desk.
3. Why don’t you **come over** this weekend?
4. A wave of sadness **came over** me.
5. He **comes over** every Sunday.
6. The cat **came over** to be petted.

**Tip:** Inviting a friend? “**Wanna come over?**” is friendly and casual.

---

## come along

> **Meaning**: Accompany, progress, or appear.  
> **日本語**：一緒に来る、進む、現れる。

### More Examples

1. **Come along** with us!
2. How’s the project **coming along**?
3. A taxi finally **came along**.
4. She asked if I wanted to **come along**.
5. Things are **coming along** nicely.
6. An opportunity like this doesn’t **come along** often.

**Tip:** Check progress by asking “**How’s it coming along?**”

## Wrap‑Up

- **come in** ➜ enter
- **come out** ➜ appear / be released
- **come up** ➜ arise / approach
- **come across** ➜ find by chance / give an impression
- **come over** ➜ visit informally
- **come along** ➜ accompany / progress
